Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a vibrant and flavorful Middle Eastern and North African dish featuring eggs poached in a spiced tomato sauce. This one-pan wonder makes for a perfect breakfast, brunch, or even dinner option. The combination of aromatic spices, fresh vegetables, and perfectly cooked eggs creates a comforting and satisfying meal that's both nutritious and delicious. Traditionally served with crusty bread for dipping, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1-2 bell peppers, chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Crushed red pepper flakes (to taste)
  • 6 vine ripe tomatoes, diced (or 28 oz canned whole tomatoes or 6 cups canned diced tomatoes)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 4-6 eggs
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Crusty bread for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat a little bit of extra virgin olive oil in a large, heavy skillet or pan with a lid over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions, bell peppers, and garlic to the pan. Sauté the vegetables, stirring occasionally.
  3. Season the vegetables with kosher salt, coriander, paprika, cumin, and crushed red pepper flakes.
  4. Cook for at least 5 minutes, stirring regularly, until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add the fresh diced tomatoes and 1/2 cup of tomato sauce to the pan. If using canned tomatoes instead, add them at this stage.
  6. Bring the tomato mixture to a boil, then cover the pan and reduce heat to let it simmer for about 15 minutes.
  7. Uncover the pan and continue cooking for a few more minutes until the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency.
  8. Using the back of a spoon, create small wells or holes in the sauce for the eggs.
  9. Crack each egg and carefully nestle it into one of the wells you created in the sauce.
  10. Cover the skillet and let the eggs simmer in the sauce over medium-low heat until the egg whites have set but the yolks remain soft and somewhat runny. For harder eggs, cook a bit longer.
  11. Once the eggs are cooked to your liking, remove from heat and garnish with fresh parsley.
  12. Serve immediately while hot, preferably with crusty bread for dipping into the delicious sauce and runny egg yolks.
